RQ5 - How the quality of Open IE results were evaluated

A major challenge in Open IE systems is to evaluate the quality of the outputs. For this, several techniques were proposed to perform these task. We used five categories to classify the studies selected and map which are the most common technique used to evaluate Open IE systems.

  • ID - The ID was created in the selection process and while applying the selection criteria many studies were excluded. We decided to keep the original ID to maintain the auditability and facilitate readers to track studies in the selection process.
  • Database - shows which database index this study
  • Title - Presents the title of the study
  • Author - Presents the author list of the study
  • Paper - Presents a link for the study
  • Year - Presents the year which the study was published
  • (1) SSC - Same system comparison - compares the systems with itself, however using different configurations
  • (2) COS - Comparison with other System - compare the systems with similar systems previously proposed
  • (3) NM - Numeric Metrics - Use numeric metrics, such as, precision and recall. Usually these metrics are obtained using an oracle dataset (previously annotated)
  • (4) HE - Human Evaluation - Use humans to evaluate the quality of results
  • (5) EA - Error analisys - Analyzes the errors to undertand the difficulties and problems of the Open IE systems
